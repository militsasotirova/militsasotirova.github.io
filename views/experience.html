Ruminant Farm Systems Developer

November 2018 - PRESENT

Cornell University

I am designing, developing, maintaining, and testing a Python application for nutrient flow through a farm system. I collaborate with student team members and professors from universities nationwide. I have multiple roles in this project, one being the technical lead of the data management team: earlier this year, I introduced the usage of databases in the project to store input and output data. I am also involved in programming model developments, mentoring new team members, and performing code reviews.

×

RuFaS is a simulation written in Python that models nutrient flow through a farm system based on user inputs as well as published research. There are four main modules: animal, soil, manure, and crop, and I primarily work in the animal module. Throughout my time on this project, I have learned a lot. This is an interdisciplinary project, as it is a computer simulation of an animal/farm system. However, even though I have no background in animal or farm science, I have been able to contribute a significant amount to the code base for the simulation:

  • I am currently the project leader of the data management team. We are using SQLite to store input data as well as persist results for future review (comparison of two runs, version to version comparisons, etc.) I lead meetings on topic and assign weekly tasks to the members of the team.
  • I restructured the entire animal module so that it takes more user input on the pens in the farm, the life cycle of the animals, and the general animal management strategy the user would like to simulate. Another outcome of this restructuring was that it more modular in nature, making understanding the code easier.
  • I coded the ration formulation as well as manure excretion component calculations for lactating cows.
  • I coded the output files for the animal module, as well as added a feature for a different style of input to the simulation that is more intuitive for the general user.
  • The project has an emphasis on documentation, as we want future developers to be able to easily contribute and users to be able to understand exactly what the simulation is doing.

I presented my work in August, 2019 and August, 2020 at the RuFaS annual meeting to an audience of programmers, the general leaders of the project, and people from the industry who will use the final product in their company policy decisions.


Throughout my time working on this project I have learned a lot about communication. People from universities all over the country work on different aspects of RuFaS and communicating our progress and issues is very important. I attend two biweekly meetings, one being a programmers meeting where we give general updates as well as bring up any issues that are relevant to the whole simulation, and the other being for the overall animal module where we discuss developments in algorithms for calculations.
